Karen

Illustrations

Overview

Context

I learned that school is only there to provide me with basic skills, and it was really on me if I wanted to improve what I learned. More specifically, I wanted to develop a “style” for my design and illustrations.

Solution Overview

I decided to create daily illustrations in my free time to find my style and also to become more familiar with Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, and Figma.

Considerations

How will I keep myself motivated? What if I lose inspiration? How will I track my progress? How will I keep myself accountable?

I turned to Instagram.* This platform allows me to easily track my progress because each photo I post will be added to my profile page. Instagram also has an art community that I hoped would keep me inspired and motivated by seeing different art styles, projects, and challenges. Since this is a public space, others will also be able to see that I’m attempting daily illustrations, which gives me some level of liability if I fail to deliver my daily illustrations.

I've archived several posts and moved them to a new platform. They can be found here.

Setting Up the Project

The setup process is so important in making sure I have ideas in mind. So, I brainstormed. I used a page of my sketchbook to doodle a couple of ideas for my illustrations, such as fruits, musical references, people, etc.

The Beginning

At the beginning, I followed the prompts from my brainstorming session. I started by drawing fruits and foods in which these fruits can be found. The first couple of days allowed me to familiarize myself with the tools on Adobe Illustrator and Figma.

This wasn’t my first time using the tools, so it didn’t take long before I was comfortable with the software again. I wanted to push myself a little. I started exploring tools I wasn’t familiar with, such as gradients and pathfinder. Eventually, these became my favourite tools and I use them on almost all my illustrations now.

Inspiration

Like I imagined, Instagram's art community inspired me to try new things. I saw a lot of architecture and isometric illustrations from some artists that I follow and this made me curious about perspective drawing on Illustrator. I watched Satori Graphic's video on isometric illustrations on YouTube and then started playing around with this style.

I've also seen some posts on Instagram that are #drawthisinyourstyle (#dtiys) challenges. These challenges are particularly helpful on days where I lack inspiration because they give me a prompt and I get to play around with a different style as well. I've also incorporated some elements from the challenge into my daily illustrations.

Outcomes

This isn’t my first time attempting to create work on a daily basis. The results were always the same -- I am extremely motivated at the beginning, but feel burnt out halfway into the month.

I lose inspiration and motivation to continue.

Creating something every day isn’t something that works for me. I understand the benefits of improving bit by bit, but I find it difficult to create something that I’m proud of and also pushes me to learn. In my past attempts (including this one), I’ve had moments where I kept up the streak of constant work at the expense of its quality.

Challenge Adjustments

This personal project isn’t over. This time around, I decided to try something new. I set a goal to make at least one illustration a week. I could do more, but I couldn’t make anything less than one illustration.

I also decided not to restrict myself with a pre-determined color palette. Using new color schemes is exciting and inspiring, which are feelings I want to remain for the duration of this project.

Where's the finish line?

As of right now, my goal is to create 100 pieces of work for this project (not including my daily ones that I’ve already done).

What I've Learned So Far

Stop comparing

This whole experience reminded me that everybody learns at a different pace; some might need more time than others, but overall, we have to work hard to achieve results and reach our goals. It taught me to recognize my accomplishments and take pride in how far I’ve come, which is a mindset I hope to carry over to other aspects of my life.

Find what works for you

30, 100, and 365 day challenges are common and there are several people who succeed in completing the challenge! After several of my failed attempts, I needed to try something that works for me and my lifestyle.
