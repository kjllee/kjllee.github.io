ConnectU App Design

Overview

Problem

The Global Business and Digital Arts (GBDA) program takes place in Stratford, Ontario (a remote campus of the University of Waterloo). Since Stratford has an older demographic and lacks student life, most students decide to live in Kitchener, Waterloo. It became common for students to leave right after classes end, which led to a lack of community among GBDA students.

Solution Overview

ConnectU is an app created to allow users to connect with one another through common interests. Unlike other apps ConnectU is exclusively for Stratford students to get to know each other more.

Design Process

Ecosystem Analysis

We had to get a better understanding of the current status of the Stratford community through research and looked more into the town itself, transportation options, and where students decided to live.

We also wanted to get students' opinions about the Stratford community. Through interviews and surveys, we learned that they value community because it helps with a sense of belonging, but they feel distant from other students on Stratford campus because of the limited time spent with one another.

What's Being Done?

​This situation is not unique to Stratford, so we took a look at some other remote and commuter-heavy campuses.

User Journeys

After researching and conducting some user research, we wanted to summarize our data with an experience map that shows how our users are feeling throughout a school term.

We had 2 main users: students who live in Stratford and students who live in Waterloo.

Wireframes

Unlike most case studies, my team did not come up with a solution ourselves. Instead, our project manager (Jason Luong) had a solution in mind that had to bring it to life. He presented his idea as “something similar to a dating app, where students can bond over common interests". That was the beginning of ConnectU.

With that idea in mind, we brainstormed features that we wanted to include, such as a discover page to meet new people. Then, we started creating a general app flow on a whiteboard and then transferred our ideas on Figma. After some low-fidelity wireframes were created, we asked our peers to do some simple tasks to determine areas of our prototype that could be confusing or unclear.

Final Design

The final design was created on Figma, which allowed us to make the app interactive.

Figma Prototype

This app does have the potential for building community among Stratford students. There are a lot of talented and curious individuals in the GBDA program and ConnectU allows students to bond over similar interests and learning new skills outside of the classroom.

Future Improvements

Accessibility
  • Is the font size and buttons large enough for users to press?
  • Do the colours contrast enough for those who are colour-blind?
Tutors
  • What would this app look like on the tutor’s end?
  • What would the onboarding be like for these users?
  • What if a user wants to be a "student" and tutor?

My Takeaways

Speak Up!

I learned about the importance of arguing for (or against) ideas and then listening to my group member’s inputs with an open mind. We all want the best for the project, so it’s important to provide your input and be open to feedback and new ideas.

Prototyping

This project allowed me to learn a lot about working with Figma. Making static screens interactive made it easier for the entire team (and beta users) to envision the navigation of the app.
